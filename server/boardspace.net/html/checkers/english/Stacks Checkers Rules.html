The Stacks Checkers Variant

Executive Summary:  

Stacks is based on Bashni, with these significant differences:
  • a king is any stack with 2 pieces the same color on top.  Kings are created (or destroyed) dynamically throughout the game.
  • back-and-forth capture is permitted.
Conseqently, there are always 12 pieces of each color in play.  Because of the back and forth capturing when to stacks are adjacent, complex interactions can occur.

Full Rules:
  • the board size is 8x8, initially with 12 men per side
  • men move forward diagonally.  Men that move into the last row become kings by adding a second man to make a stack of 2.
  • men capture adjacent men of the other color, forward or backward, by jumping if there is an empty landing square immediately after.
  • captures can be either forward or backward, and are mandatory as long as more captures are possible.
  • a man capturing and landing on the king row is promoted to King immediately, and can continue capturing as a King.
  • kings "fly"; they move forward or backward and can skip empry spaces before and after a jumped piece.

Conditions for a Draw: 

  • Mutual agreement of the players. 
  • The same position occurs three times
  • Both players have kings, and both players have made 25 king moves without captures or moving a man.
  • Both players have 3 or fewer pieces, and both players have made 16 king moves without captures or moving a man.
Stacks checkers is unique to boardspace, inspired by intermediate rule sets while I was interpreting unclear versions of the rules for Bashni
